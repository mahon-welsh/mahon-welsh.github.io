EQ Vs. IQ

What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional intelligence, measured by EQ describes an individual's ability to perform several functions vital for mantaining a healthy mind. These functions include but are not limited to: Empathy, emotional regulation, communication/collaboration, and exploration of the self.

How is EQ different to IQ?

EQ is different to IQ in that IQ is a flat measure of ability to perform in tasks. Most Iq tests measure an individual’s performance answering complex questions that often involve mathematics, reading comprehension and context-based elements. These questions are designed to test the flexibility and fortitude of your mind when it comes to tackling questions that often require input from multiple areas of the mind. This can be a good measure of someone's working ability for challenges based purely on intelligence. EQ on the other hand, measures our ability to understand ourselves and others. This ability is a crucial part of what makes up someone’s overall intelligence. Another important difference of EQ is that it is significantly easier to work on than IQ. While not impossible, most research suggests that IQ is based on several developmental and early-life factors, and mature individuals' IQ does not tend to fluctuate much over time. EQ however, can be provably trained by employing many different methods. Practices of mindfulness, like meditation or journaling can help us understand more about ourselves, and the people around us, and how best to approach things that we've previously struggled with. Thinking about self-mastery allows us to consider why we feel the way we do about situations, and perhaps how we can alter the way we view negative stimulus.

Why is EQ Important?

Emotional intelligence is important in all walks of life. Funnily enough, everything gets easier when you feel happy, organised, and aware of things that could challenge that. It is also true that socialisation, collaboration, and teamwork are all important parts of our lives. In the very narrow example of the tech workspace, it doesn’t particularly matter if you are a genius coder if you struggle to communicate and work with a team. Something like coding requires clear dialogue between members of a team, which involves being able to consciously hear people out, retain their ideas, and work with them to build upon them further. The same is true of being able to confidently and politely express your ideas to others, for them in turn to hear you out. While many people point to the importance of Iq for things like managerial and coordinative positions, I would argue that Eq is important throughout all levels of any workplace, especially something like coding in a team.
