Identity, Values, and Strengths

Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

I struggled to come up with an example but I reckon the best one is as follows. I used to be very close to a particular group of friends, back before I really came out of my shell I relied on interaction with that group. As I started to come into my own more I realised that those people made me miserable and we never really had any fun together. Admittedly, I parted ways from this group after doing some unkind stuff, which I regret to this day. With that considered, leaving that group of friends still taught me to seek out people who I value, and who value me. It also gave me valuable experience in making new connections, which provided me much needed confidence.

Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

I have spent a lot of time with many different groups and different types of people growing up. I feel like I’ve taken bits and pieces from all of these different connections to form a well rounded and accepting mindset. I’ve spent time hanging around all kinds of delinquents, to mainly associating with academics. What I’ve come to find is that everyone has a story, everyone has their strengths, and everyone has something that makes them special.

Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

I would say one of my strengths is sociability. I find it easy to connect with people and always enjoy hearing more about them. As such, I find myself far more committed and capable of meeting deadlines and requirements if I’m putting in that effort for others. I also believe I have a pretty quick mind, and if I manage to remove myself from distractions, then I can be an extremely capable and efficient learner.

Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

I have ADHD + Autism, meaning that generally being quick to things comes with its fair share of challenges. I definitely struggle with procrastination, self motivation, focus and willpower. However, I hope to work on managing these things through our learning in the human skills units!

Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

The main group work I have experienced has been stuff in highschool, which always came to some circumstance where no one was willing to do the work or communicate. Back then, just doing all of it was easy, and often a way to make some money off of the people in my group. However, Devacademy will obviously provide challenging and unfamiliar group work where this strategy will not work. I’ve become a far more cooperative person since then, and I look forward to working with groups where interaction, planning and communication are encouraged.
