Human Skills Reflections

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the Human Skills learning?

I feel like I’ve learnt alot about different personal triggers for anger and frustration, and how to best mitigate these things. I also feel like I’ve done a lot of thinking about settings and stimulus around me to get into my most productive state. Lastly, I feel a lot less embarrassed about feeling incompetent, or ‘behind’ my peers. I understand that there's lots to learn and even if I feel like I’m not doing the greatest job, the only way to get better is to keep coding, and keep not knowing stuff.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

As the human skills units have reiterated, a great member of a team is someone who can elevate the vibe of the people around them. I feel like values are important in that having strong values is bound to rub off on teammates. Holding value in ideas like collaboration, openness, helping out others, etc is likely to encourage a team where everyone feels comfortable working with one another, which allows for much more shit to get done! Empathy and self-awareness also play an important role, it’s important to not just be kind and accommodating to others in a team, but also to be capable of giving yourself the same treatment. If you can’t be kind to yourself, every minor issue or roadblock becomes far more of a struggle.

What has surprised you the most about the Human Skills learning?

Since I have a background in psych, I definitely thought that there was going to be less for me to learn in the Human skills units. However, there were heaps of cool resources to check out! While I definitely did already know about plenty of the concepts we covered, there was some stuff, or some perspectives on these concepts that were completely new to me.

What were the most challenging aspects of the Human Skills learning?

While not super challenging, I’d say the most strenuous part was actually putting the knowledge into practice. I found myself consciously using techniques such as self-mastery and awareness when approaching frustration not just with course work, but other aspects of my life. The other slightly difficult one to keep on top of was trying to write more detailed journal entries. Brief notes about my day or days coming up is something I’ve done for years at this point, but then taking the time to go back and assign understanding to how I feel about things has been really helpful, just easy to forget to keep in the habit of.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on Human Skills learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

I think it’s important because a lot of people with an interest in something like coding/web development are known for being less social people. I think it’s important to encourage an environment like Dev where everyone feels safe to reach out and just have yarns in general. The other big reason that I feel like the Human skills stuff is important to cover is how easy it is to get frustrated with something like coding. It’s really valuable to have techniques and concepts to help out with these negative feelings, and how to bounce back from some evil-ass code mental block.

Does the time you spent studying Human Skills learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

I don’t think that Human skills learning felt like a waste of time. I felt like I got plenty of value out of it despite being very familiar with some of the learning such as the content on neuroplasticity. I think that the content, even if already known, serves as a great reminder to uphold the principles we covered. I feel like reminding myself that it’s ok to give myself a minute, that each time I do any coding I’m actively strengthening my brain's ability to think like a coder, among other things are really helpful to keep in mind.
