My Learning Plan!

What is your long term goal or career pathway?

My long term goal is to have a high demand, flexible skill set in coding and development, in order to be as employable as I can be while also being able to make awesome things in my spare time! I want to be able to perform competently in different roles not only to be a valuable employee, but also so I can spread my wings and not box myself into one position.

A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning

In terms of strengths, when I’m applying myself and giving myself a proper chance, I can normally apply and understand content pretty quickly. While I can definitely take in new information well, I struggle a lot with motivation, time management, focus, etc.

What skills (non-technical/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I definitely want to put as much practice as I can into managing my time and planning my study effectively. I’ve typically never used planners or written anything down and just relied on memory and common sense for any kind of study. I’ve definitely found through my work at Dev that this is absolutely not feasible for this kind of learning. As such, I’m doing my best to stick with the organisational tools that Dev suggested, as well as figuring out what personal methods work best for me.

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme.

A big worry of mine has always been letting down teammates in group activities, or being the weakest link. I’m committed to writing every date down, almost constantly having access to discord or other channels of communication, and pushing work I’m 100% confident in well before deadlines (in case anything goes wrong!). I’m also committed to be respectful of my fellow learners’ cultures, beliefs, interests, and identities. I’m also committed to using appropriate channels and means to ask for help, which is something I’ve been traditionally poor at, but Dev is a very welcoming space!

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way

As it has been covered extensively, I’m aware that there are several ways to ask for help at Devacademy. Before asking for help, it’s best to go over the content again, as well as make sure there aren’t any obvious typos in my code. The next best bet is to check out the foundations helpdesk channel on discord, as it is likely that someone else will have already had a similar issue at some point. If I can’t find anything that helps me sort out my issue, then I can ask my home group, the foundations help desk, or a facilitator.

A description of what you expect from the Facilitation team

I expect the facilitation team to be respectful and kind to the learners at Devacademy. I expect that within my facilitator will keep our home group chat up to date with anything I’d need to know on the fly, or if there’s something important to attend (it’s up to me to keep up with my messages though!). I expect that within reason, at reasonable times during a work day that I’m able to shoot a brief question through to them, or let them know if there's some major issue with the work/in life that needs some kind of resolution.

Any scheduling information such as block-out times when you are committed to other things. For example, you might have whānau commitments like school drop-offs. It's important to plan these in.
